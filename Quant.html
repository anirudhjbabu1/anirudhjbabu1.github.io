The Architecture

1. Statistical Arbitrage: Pairs Trading (Beginner-Intermediate) Instead of predicting one stock, you predict the relationship between two. The Goal: Find two stocks that are historically correlated (e.g., ICICI Bank and HDFC Bank). When the spread between them widens significantly, you short the "expensive" one and buy the "cheap" one, betting they will converge. Key Skills: Cointegration (Augmented Dickey-Fuller test), Z-score calculation, and Mean Reversion. Dataset: Daily or hourly data from yfinance or NSE historical archives. Implementation: Use Python (Statsmodels, Pandas). 2. Limit Order Book (LOB) Imbalance (Intermediate-Advanced) This is a classic "Quant Researcher" project that bridges the gap between ML and HFT. The Goal: Use Level-2 market data (the "Order Book") to predict short-term price movements (the next 10–60 seconds). The ML Angle: Train a Random Forest or LightGBM on "Order Flow Imbalance" (OFI) and "Bid-Ask Spread" to determine if a "buy" or "sell" wall is about to break. Key Skills: Data cleaning of high-frequency tick data, feature engineering (VWAP, micro-price), and avoiding "Look-ahead bias." Dataset: Crypto tick data (Binance/Coinbase API) or LOB datasets from Kaggle. 3. Reinforcement Learning for Execution (Advanced) Move away from "Signal" and focus on "Execution." The Goal: Train an RL agent (PPO or DQN) to execute a large order (e.g., buying 10,000 shares) without moving the market price too much. The ML Angle: The "Reward" is the difference between your execution price and the market VWAP (Volume Weighted Average Price). Key Skills: OpenAI Gym environments, Reward function design, and Market Impact modeling. Implementation: Stable-Baselines3 in Python. 4. Sentiment-Based "Alpha" Factor (Alternative Data) Show that you can handle unstructured data—a huge trend in 2026. The Goal: Scrape financial news or "FinTwit" (Financial Twitter/X) to create a sentiment score for a specific sector. The ML Angle: Use a Transformer (FinBERT) to classify news as Bullish/Bearish. Correlate this score with the next day's returns to see if it’s a valid "Alpha" signal. Key Skills: NLP, API scraping, and Cross-correlation analysis.
